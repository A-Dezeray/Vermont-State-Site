The name "Vermont" is derived from the French words "vert mont," meaning "green mountain." This name was given by the French explorer Samuel de Champlain when he encountered the region's prominent and lush mountain ranges. The Green Mountains are a central and defining feature of the state's landscape, which led to the nickname "The Green Mountain State." This moniker reflects the state's verdant, mountainous terrain that remains a symbol of Vermont's natural beauty." Source
